Eric Bohanon

Reflections

Rhythm without time

Inspired by Debussy, this piece makes every attempt to remove the aspect of time from its rhythm. Due to the tempo rubato (meaning the performer has freedom of expression in terms of tempo and how it can change throughout the piece), and the slow nature of the piece, people may find it hard to find any particular rhythm. The focus of the piece resides in the different sounds created by the notes running together (this piece requires heavy pedal from the piano player). The dynamics also play an important part, highlighting certain sections, and drawing the listener into a thoughtful place in others via soffter tones. Crescendos and descendos also provide interesting patterns that play unique from performer to performer.
